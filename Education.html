Why Electrical Engineering?

Electrical Engineering

The foundation for creating innovative diagnostic and therapeutic technologies

My Career Goals Related to My Major

My career goal has always been to improve or create diagnostic and therapeutic technology. A pivotal experience early in life made me realize just how much the medical industry needs innovation in these areas. That moment sparked a deep curiosity, I spent hours researching different paths to make an impact, from law and medical school to scientific research. Eventually, I discovered biomedical engineering and knew it was the right direction for me.

Although the University of Alabama doesn’t offer a biomedical engineering major, I explored different programs until I found the right fit: electrical engineering with a minor in biochemistry. Electrical engineering gives me the technical foundation to work with diagnostic and therapeutic devices, while biochemistry ensures I meet core prerequisites like organic chemistry. Together, these fields prepare me to pursue a master’s degree in biomedical engineering and bring meaningful change to patient care.

Did you start as an electrical engineering major?

No. My first year I was a triple major in Mechanical Engineering, Chemical Engineering (w/ a Biomedical Concentration), and Business with minors in EPIC and Manufacturing. It was overwhelming, and when I looked at my future schedule, I realized I wouldn’t have any free time or space for student organizations. My degree then became a Mechanical Engineering and Business double major with a minor in biochemistry, Manufacturing, and EPIC (so much less work, I know). And then I finally came to my senses and it became electrical engineering with a minor in biochemistry and EPIC. This allowed me to realign my goals and cut out what wasn’t necessary, giving me time for hobbies and involvement outside academics. Now I feel confident in my degree choice.

Engineering Positive & Intentional Change

EPIC

What is EPIC?

“The mission of the Engineering Positive and Intentional Change (EPIC) Scholars Program centers on developing engineering and computer science leaders who understand the impact of social and societal issues in the engineering and computer science professions and dedicate themselves to leading efforts to find sustainable solutions to broad, complex, contemporary societal issues facing the state of Alabama, the nation, and the world. The EPIC Scholars Program is a recognized partner program of the Honors College and adopts an inquiry-driven approach to broad, complex, contemporary social and societal issues, that complements the technical education of engineering and computer science students. Only EPIC Scholars who successfully complete the EPIC Scholars Program in good standing as an Honors student will be recognized with the Minor in Engineering Positive and Intentional Change.” — University of Alabama

EPIC and Its Role in Biomedical Engineering

The EPIC Scholars Program has been one of the most transformative and grounding experiences of my undergraduate journey. More than just an academic program, EPIC challenges students to approach engineering not only with technical excellence, but with a mindset rooted in empathy, ethics, and social responsibility. As someone planning to pursue biomedical engineering, I’ve found that this lens is not just useful, it’s necessary. The field demands more than equations and prototypes, it demands a deep understanding of the people behind the problems we are trying to solve. Through EPIC, I’ve learned how to question the status quo, examine social systems around technological solutions, and prioritize the long term impact of my work. Whether it’s a medical device or a diagnostic tool, I now view every innovation within a larger human context.

Technical Solutions + Social Impact

I can develop technical solutions that address real world societal challenges while creating meaningful value for communities.

Community Engagement

I’ve gained skills to foster community involvement and prioritize social responsibility alongside technical innovation.

This combination prepares me for roles that require both strong technical knowledge and a commitment to ethical, socially conscious engineering.

Why I Joined EPIC?

This program has given me a space to channel my personal experiences into meaningful purpose. Growing up in a household shaped by chronic pain, I witnessed how slow, expensive, and inequitable the medical system can be. Both of my parents live with chronic ailments, and their healthcare journeys were full of obstacles and delays. I’ve also faced similar challenges, living with chronic pain and waiting years for a diagnosis. These experiences drew me to biomedical engineering, but EPIC helped me realize that technical skills alone aren’t enough. To truly make a difference, you have to understand the people affected to create impactful solutions. EPIC teaches me to look beyond data to consider communities, systems, and the power engineers have to create change on a large scale.

My Undergraduate Experience in EPIC

Beyond its academic and ethical foundation, EPIC has been the source of some of the strongest relationships I’ve built in college. It’s a unique community of people who care deeply, not just about their majors or careers, but about each other and the world around them. Through shared projects and discussions, I’ve developed friendships that extend far beyond the classroom. One of my closest friends from EPIC even became my roommate. The support I’ve found has helped me navigate college’s ups and downs and stay focused on the engineer I want to become. Today, I proudly serve as an EPIC Ambassador, helping organize events and foster the same growth and community EPIC gave me.
